// THESIS UPDATE — MARCH 2026
Carlit has re-rated sharply — ¥1,008 to ¥2,775, +175%. The market is beginning to understand what it owns. But this is phase one of the re-rating, not the end of it.

At ¥2,775, Carlit trades at approximately 22× P/E on FY2026 guided earnings of ¥125.8 EPS, and around 15.4× EV/EBIT. Those are no longer embarrassingly cheap multiples — but they are still meaningfully below where this business should trade. AMPAC, the structural US equivalent, was acquired in January 2024 at approximately ~40× EV/EBIT. Chemring, a broader energetics business with less monopoly depth, trades at 14.3× EV/EBITDA — implying a higher EV/EBIT. NOF Corporation, Carlit's own downstream customer, trades at roughly 20–25× P/E.

The critical point: the market is still pricing FY2026 earnings, but the FY2027 inflection has not been priced. The ×3 AP capacity expansion completes this month. Full utilisation of that capacity drives operating profit from ¥3,500M to a management-guided ¥4,200M in FY2027 — a 20% EBIT jump. At 22× on FY2027 earnings (~¥175 EPS), the stock should trade at roughly ¥3,850. At 25× — the low end of a genuine defense monopoly premium — it's ¥4,375. The NOF propellant qualification, if achieved, is pure upside on top.
